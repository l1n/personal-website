Jos Johnson As one who favors Green, I feel you have misunderstood the philosophy behind it. Green focuses on several things, including nature, growth, instinct (which it shares with Red), wisdom, and interconnectedness (which it shares with White).

As others have mentioned, reaching in and directly changing someone's nature is more Blue's domain (or perhaps Black), and is actually quite antithetical to Green's focus on embracing your inherent nature. In fact, Blue and Green are "enemy" colors for precisely this reason: Blue seeks to change everything into what it "should" be; Green desires that everything fulfill it's natural purpose.

I feel that Green's response would be to show you the consequences of your actions: on yourself, on others, and on the world around you. But more than just showing, Green would make you *live* with those consequences, so that you would feel on an instinctual level that what you did was wrong, regardless of whether you understand it intellectually or philosophically (which is Blue or White's domain). That way, you would gain wisdom, and grow as a person.
